Privacy Policy for Central 365

   

Last Updated: 17 June 2026

   

This Privacy Policy governs the use of the mobile application Central 365 ("the Application"), created and operated by Ian Wilson ("we," "us," or "our"). The Application is designed as an enterprise-focused tool that authenticates users through their organization's identity provider.

   

By downloading, accessing, or using the Application, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with these terms, please do not use the Application.

   
   

1. Information Collection and Use

   

A. Personal Data and Identity

   

The Application does not host, manage, or store your personal user credentials, profile databases, or private identity records.

   
           
  • Enterprise Authentication: Access to the Application is managed exclusively via Microsoft Entra ID. Your login credentials, email address, password, and enterprise authentication tokens are handled securely directly by Microsoft. They are never transmitted to, viewed by, or stored on private servers managed by Ian Wilson.
    •    
   

B. Device Permissions and Data Minimisation

   

In alignment with strict data minimisation principles, Central 365 does not access, collect, or monitor the following device features:

   
           
  • Location Data: The Application does not track your physical location (neither precise nor coarse).
    •        
  • Media and Device Access: The Application requests no access to your device camera, photo gallery, microphone, or local contacts file.
    •    
   

C. Telemetry and Analytics Data

   

The Application automatically collects minimal, non-personally identifiable diagnostic data to maintain core application performance.

   
           
  • Service Used: We use Microsoft Azure App Insights to collect performance analytics.
    •        
  • Data Collected: This tracking includes anonymised device models, operating system versions, app crash logs, and feature usage statistics.
    •        
  • Purpose: This data is compiled solely to identify bugs, improve software stability, and ensure operational health. It cannot be used to trace back to or identify any individual user.
    •    
   
   

2. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

   

We do not sell, trade, or rent user data to third parties. Data processing is restricted exclusively to the following systems:

   
           
  • Infrastructure and Telemetry: Analytical logs and telemetry infrastructure are processed entirely via Microsoft Azure.
    •        
  • Advertisements: The Application is 100% ad-free. It contains no code, SDKs, or trackers linked to third-party ad brokers or marketing networks.
    •        
  • Payments and Transactions: All financial actions and subscriptions are executed through the standard native Apple App Store In-App Purchase framework. We do not process, collect, or store payment cards, bank account details, or billing addresses.
    •    
   
   

3. Data Retention and Deletion

   

A. Data Retention Policy

   

Because the Application operates without dedicated private databases or cloud-hosted user profiles, we do not store persistent personal data (Storage Duration: None). Telemetry logs within Microsoft Azure App Insights are automatically cycle-deleted according to standard enterprise retention windows.

   

B. Account Deletion (Enterprise Managed)

   

Because user identities are managed externally via your employer or organization's Microsoft Entra ID ecosystem, standard in-app account creation handles do not apply.

   
           
  • If you wish to revoke the Application’s access to your enterprise profile, or if you need your identity records removed, you must contact your organization's internal IT department or system administrator to modify your enterprise directory permissions.

4. Children's Privacy

   

The Application is intended strictly for business, workplace, and enterprise use. We do not knowingly collect or solicit personal information from individuals under the age of 13. If you believe a child has accessed the platform through corporate credentials, please contact us immediately.
